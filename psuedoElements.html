Pseudo Elements

Pseudo elements are the first letter within an element, first line within a paragraph, "before", and "after." They are mainly used to modify the style of the first letter or line of the paragraph. To do this, in the "style" tags of the "head" section of your HTML document put the name of the element (in this case p for paragraph) followed by a colon and then the pseudo element (either first-letter, first-line, before, or after). In this case we did: p:first-letter {font-weight:bold; font-size:30px; }.
